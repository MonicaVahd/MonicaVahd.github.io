About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Ottawa, supervised by Professor Abdulmotaleb El Saddik at the Multimedia Communications Research Lab (MCRLab). My dissertation, FAIR: A Flexible, Agentic, Intelligent, Real-Time Coach for Multimodal Posture Correction, develops a novel agentic AI system that integrates LLM-driven decision-making, multi-agent orchestration (CrewAI, Agent-to-Agent protocol), and reinforcement learning for real-time personalized fitness coaching.

My work spans agentic AI architectures, multimodal digital twins, real-time perception–action systems, and reinforcement learning for human–AI interaction. Before my doctoral studies I worked for nine years as a Software Developer at Tamin ICT, designing 40+ enterprise applications for insurance and financial sectors. I hold a GPA of 8.9/10 and am continuously funded by the University of Ottawa's Admission, Special Merit, and International Doctoral Scholarships.
